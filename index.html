Our Mission

Money has always moved
at the speed of banks.
We built what comes next.

We exist to make international payments instantaneous, easily verifiable, and accessible at a fraction of traditional cost, connecting businesses and people across every border.

1.0
The First Revolution: Banks
Banks gave the world a trusted place to store and move money. For the first time, businesses could transact across cities and borders with confidence. But trust came at a price: slow settlement times, heavy intermediaries, and access reserved for those with the right relationships. Moving money internationally still takes days and costs a small fortune.
2.0
The Second Revolution: The Internet
The internet digitized money and made payments faster. PayPal, cards, and mobile apps brought financial services to a screen near you. But underneath, the rails were still the same: legacy banking infrastructure built for a pre-digital world. International transfers still route through correspondent banks, still carry high fees, and still take days to settle. The interface changed. The system did not.
3.0
The Third Revolution: The Internet of Money
The third revolution is the internet of money, a global open financial layer where value moves like information. Programmable, borderless, and verifiable by anyone. Money can now settle in seconds without asking permission from a bank. A business in Bogotá can pay a supplier in Miami the same way it sends an email. Stablecoins, smart contracts, and public blockchains are the infrastructure of this new era. Puent3 was built to operate at its frontier.

Understanding the
new rails of money.

Stablecoins and blockchain payments are the foundation of what Puent3 does. We believe an informed client is a confident client. Here is what you need to know.

The Basics

What is a Stablecoin?

A stablecoin is a type of digital currency whose value is pegged to a stable asset, typically the US dollar. Unlike Bitcoin or other cryptocurrencies, stablecoins do not fluctuate in price. One USDC is always worth one US dollar. This makes them ideal for payments. You know exactly what you are sending and what the recipient will receive.

How It Works

What is a Stablecoin?

Stablecoins are regulated digital currencies pegged to the value of a real-world asset, most commonly the US dollar. USDC, issued by Circle, is one of the stablecoins Puent3 uses to move your money, alongside others depending on your corridor and currency. Every dollar-backed stablecoin is supported 1:1 by dollar reserves held in regulated financial institutions, making them stable, transparent, and fully auditable.

Getting Started

How Do I Convert Fiat to Stablecoins?

Converting traditional currency (fiat) to stablecoins is straightforward. You can use a regulated exchange such as Coinbase, Kraken, or Binance to purchase stablecoins using a bank transfer or debit card. Once purchased, you transfer them to your Puent3 wallet and you are ready to send payments. The process takes minutes and fees are typically minimal.

Key Terms
Blockchain
A public, distributed ledger that permanently records every transaction. Think of it as a global spreadsheet that anyone can read, but no one can alter. Every transfer made through Puent3 is recorded on-chain and verifiable by anyone.
Wallet
A digital wallet is an account that holds your stablecoins. It has a unique address (similar to a bank account number) and is used to send and receive stablecoin payments. Your Puent3 client wallet is dedicated exclusively to you and fully controlled by Puent3 on your behalf.
On-Ramp
The process of converting traditional fiat currency into a stablecoin. On-ramps are typically provided by regulated exchanges or financial institutions and allow clients to fund their stablecoin wallet before sending payments through Puent3.
Off-Ramp
The process of converting USDC back into local fiat currency and delivering it to a recipient's bank account. Off-ramps are handled by regulated financial partners and enable recipients to receive local currency without needing to hold or manage digital assets themselves.
KYC / AML
Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) are regulatory requirements that financial services companies must follow. KYC verifies who you are. AML ensures the money being moved does not come from illegal sources. Puent3 requires KYC for all clients and screens every transaction for AML compliance automatically.
Settlement
Settlement is the moment a payment is finalized and funds are confirmed as delivered. Traditional wire transfers settle in 1 to 5 business days. USDC transactions on blockchain settle in seconds to minutes, with full cryptographic confirmation. There is no waiting, no uncertainty, and no reversal once confirmed.
Ethereum
The second-largest blockchain by adoption, launched in 2015. Ethereum introduced smart contracts, self-executing code that runs on the blockchain without any intermediary. This innovation made it possible to build financial applications, stablecoins, and payment protocols directly on a public blockchain. Many major stablecoins run natively on Ethereum, making it one of the foundational networks for modern digital payments.
Solana
A high-performance blockchain designed for speed and scale. Solana can process thousands of transactions per second at a fraction of a cent per transaction, making it one of the fastest and most cost-efficient blockchains available. It is one of the networks used to move stablecoins in modern payment infrastructure.

Frequently Asked Questions

Have Questions?

How long does a payment take?

Most payments complete within minutes, depending on the destination country and payment method. Blockchain settlements are near-instant.

What are your fees?

Our fees are transparent and disclosed upfront before every transaction is confirmed. The rate you see is the rate you pay, with no hidden charges. Contact us to discuss your volume and use case.

Do I need crypto knowledge?

No! We handle all blockchain complexity. You simply send us fiat (USD, CAD, COP, etc.) and your recipient receives their preferred currency. The stablecoin technology works behind the scenes.

What compliance measures do you have?

We follow strict KYC/AML regulations, use Persona for identity verification, and partner with Circle for regulated stablecoin infrastructure. All transactions are monitored and compliant with US regulations.

How do I get started?

Start by completing the registration form at the link above. You will then need to submit a set of required business and identity documents for our compliance review. Once your application is reviewed and approved, our team will contact you to complete onboarding. Please allow time for the verification process. We are committed to thorough compliance.

Which countries do you support?

We currently support payment corridors connecting Latin America, the United States, and Canada. Our network is actively expanding. Contact us to confirm whether your specific origin and destination countries are supported.
