Educational Resources

Understanding the
new rails of money.

Stablecoins and blockchain payments are the foundation of what Puent3 does. We believe an informed client is a confident client. Here is what you need to know.

The Basics

What is a Stablecoin?

A stablecoin is a type of digital currency whose value is pegged to a stable asset, typically the US dollar. Unlike Bitcoin or other cryptocurrencies, stablecoins do not fluctuate in price. One USDC is always worth one US dollar. This makes them ideal for payments. You know exactly what you are sending and what the recipient will receive.

How It Works

What is USDC?

USDC (USD Coin) is the world's leading regulated digital dollar, issued by Circle. Every USDC in circulation is backed 1:1 by US dollar reserves, held in regulated financial institutions. Circle publishes monthly attestations verifying these reserves. USDC is what Puent3 uses to move your money. Stable, transparent, and fully auditable.

Getting Started

How Do I Convert Fiat to USDC?

Converting traditional currency (fiat) to USDC is straightforward. You can use a regulated exchange such as Coinbase, Kraken, or Binance US to purchase USDC using a bank transfer or debit card. Once purchased, you transfer the USDC to your Puent3 wallet and you are ready to send payments. The process takes minutes and fees are typically less than 1%.

Key Terms
Blockchain
A public, distributed ledger that permanently records every transaction. Think of it as a global spreadsheet that anyone can read, but no one can alter. Every USDC transfer made through Puent3 is recorded on-chain and verifiable by anyone.
Wallet
A digital wallet is an account that holds your USDC. It has a unique address (similar to a bank account number) and is used to send and receive stablecoin payments. Your Puent3 client wallet is dedicated exclusively to you and fully controlled by Puent3 on your behalf.
On-Ramp
The process of converting traditional fiat currency (USD, COP, MXN, etc.) into digital currency such as USDC. On-ramps are typically provided by regulated exchanges. This is the first step to using Puent3: converting your dollars into USDC so they can move at the speed of the blockchain.
Off-Ramp
The reverse of an on-ramp: converting USDC back into local fiat currency and delivering it to a recipient bank account. Puent3 handles the off-ramp for your recipients automatically. They receive local currency in their bank account without needing to understand or interact with crypto.
KYC / AML
Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) are regulatory requirements that financial services companies must follow. KYC verifies who you are. AML ensures the money being moved does not come from illegal sources. Puent3 requires KYC for all clients and screens every transaction for AML compliance automatically.
Settlement
Settlement is the moment a payment is finalized and funds are confirmed as delivered. Traditional wire transfers settle in 1 to 5 business days. USDC transactions on blockchain settle in seconds to minutes, with full cryptographic confirmation. There is no waiting, no uncertainty, and no reversal once confirmed.
